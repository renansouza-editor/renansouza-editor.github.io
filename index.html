FAQ

Common Questions

Straight answers to the things people usually ask before working together.

Will I be charged for the free 60-second video you offered me?+

No. If I reached out to you with a free 60-second sample edit, it's completely free — no strings attached, no invoice waiting afterward. If you like the result, we can talk about ongoing work together. If it's not for you, no hard feelings — the clip is still yours to keep.

What if I need more than the included revisions?+

Extra revisions are $10 each and add one additional day to the final delivery, on top of the rounds already included in your package. If you need a faster turnaround on an extra round, that can be arranged for a higher fee — just tell me upfront and I'll confirm if it's possible.

How do we handle file sharing and communication?+

Footage comes over however is easiest for you — Google Drive, WeTransfer, or a direct link. For day-to-day communication, WhatsApp is the fastest (usually under 6 hours) and Discord works too (within 24 hours). Pick whichever you're already comfortable with.

What happens if you miss a deadline?+

If a delay is coming, I'll flag it before the deadline, not after. But if a deadline is missed without warning, your next video comes with a discount reflecting how late the delivery was and how urgent your content was — up to 50% off in the most extreme cases. You shouldn't have to chase me for updates.

Can I request a specific style or send reference videos?+

Absolutely — send me 2–3 reference videos and I'll match the pacing, tone, and structure to your brand instead of applying a generic template. Your channel should still feel like your channel.

Will my raw or unreleased footage stay private?+

Yes. Footage and streams are used only for the edit you hired me for — never reused, reposted, or shown publicly without your permission. If I'd like to feature a finished piece in my portfolio, I'll ask first.

Will you sign an NDA?+

Yes, no problem. If you're working with unreleased content, an unannounced sponsorship, or anything sensitive, I'm happy to sign an NDA before you send anything over.

How does payment work?+

For new clients, the usual structure is 50% upfront to start the edit and 50% on delivery of the approved final cut. For ongoing retainer work, billing is arranged monthly. If that doesn't fit your situation, just ask — it can be adjusted.

Who owns the final video and project files?+

You do. The final delivered video is yours to publish, monetize, and use however you'd like. Raw project files can be shared on request as well.
